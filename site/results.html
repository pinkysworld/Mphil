Temporal Behaviour

What changes once time is taken seriously

Walk-forward evaluation

The 19-window rolling evaluation produced a mean macro-F1 of 0.7908, with monthly performance ranging from 0.6690 to 0.9289. The variation is large enough to matter and suggests that a single split does not tell the full story.

Invariance and discriminability

API tokens and artifact tokens are both highly discriminative, with within-window macro-F1 values of 0.9744 and 0.9641 respectively. Behaviour counts and static PE features appear more limited in predictive value.

Leakage Control

Current ablation outcome

Random split

Controlled and permitted runs both returned macro-F1 0.8792 in the current implementation, giving a delta of 0.0000.

Chronological split

Controlled and permitted runs both returned macro-F1 0.7830, again producing a delta of 0.0000 in the present ablation pass.

This does not mean leakage is never a concern. It means that under the current filtering setup and tokenisation path, the aggregate ablation did not show measurable inflation.

Calibration

Confidence is more reliable after calibration

Uncalibrated model

Accuracy 0.9559, macro-F1 0.8996, ECE 0.1031.

Sigmoid-calibrated model

Accuracy 0.9335, macro-F1 0.9071, ECE 0.0334.

Interpretation

The calibrated model gives up a small amount of accuracy but produces substantially more trustworthy confidence scores.

Per-family ECE still varies noticeably across families, so the confidence story is not uniform across the label set.
